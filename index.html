Cover Me, Jul 2023:
In the age of automation and artificial intelligence, Cover Me serves as a groundbreaking backend application that streamlines the job application process. By leveraging the command-line interface, the application skillfully scrapes various job sites, parsing the information into neatly organized JSON files. Users have the convenience of feeding their resumes into the system, which when combined with the job data, empowers the integrated AI to craft tailored cover letters for matching job positions based on user specifications. Built using Python, various job databases, Scrapfly, and the robust capabilities of the OpenAI API, Cover Me significantly reduces the hassle and time consumed in job applications, ensuring each cover letter is personalized and pertinent.

Pinpoint, Apr 2023:
Travelling the world or just exploring from the comfort of home has never been as immersive as with Pinpoint. This 3D globe application redefines how we interact with GPS data, presenting a visually stunning and informative experience based on any given location. The core of Pinpoint is built using three.js and JavaScript, which together bring to life a vivid, interactive globe. Meanwhile, MUI components bridge the gap, facilitating seamless communication with a Node.js Express backend. The integration of Wiki and Google APIs ensures users are always presented with rich, contextual information about their chosen locations. Additionally, MongoDB serves as the robust database backbone, ensuring data integrity and speed.

City Explorer, Mar 2023:
My first born full-stack app. Although not the best I have made, still proud of this one and plan to enhance it in the future. City Explorer is the go-to application for those curious about the climatic conditions and geographical layout of any city within the USA. With a focus on simplicity and effectiveness, users can select a city of their choice, and the application promptly displays the current weather and a comprehensive map of the chosen location. Developed with the power of React combined with React-Bootstrap, the frontend delivers a responsive and intuitive user interface. The backend, powered by JavaScript, employs Axios to seamlessly connect with the LocationIQ API, ensuring accurate and timely data is always at the fingertips of the user.
